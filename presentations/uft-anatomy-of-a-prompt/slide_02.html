What Is an LLM?

"LLM" stands for Large Language Model — the engine behind ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini. In plain English:

📚 It learned from a LOT of text

It read a huge slice of the internet, books, and articles — and learned the patterns of how words fit together.

🔮 It predicts the next word

It does not "look things up." It generates the most likely next word, again and again, to build a response.

⚠️ It does not "know" facts

It produces text that sounds right. That means it can be confidently wrong — always verify what matters.

📱

Think: Autocomplete on Steroids

Your phone guesses your next word. An LLM does the same thing — but trained on billions of sentences, so it can write whole emails, lessons, and plans.

You type:

"The best part of teaching is..."

It predicts a likely finish:

"...watching a student finally get it."

Because it works by prediction, the input you give it shapes everything — which is exactly why prompts matter.
