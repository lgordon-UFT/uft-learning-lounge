"What's the best way to use AI to help draft a grievance response without it sounding generic or corporate? Every time I try, it loses the union voice entirely."

✅ Top Answer
The fix is a persona. Before asking for the draft, paste a persona that tells the AI: "You are a UFT grievance specialist. Your tone is firm, member-centered, and grounded in contract language. Never use corporate HR phrasing." Then ask for the draft. The persona pre-loads the union voice so the AI doesn't default to a generic professional style. — Maria T., UFT Communications

"Can I save my persona in Claude so I don't have to re-paste it every time I open a new conversation? It's getting tedious."

✅ Top Answer
Yes — use Claude Projects! Go to claude.ai, click "Projects" in the left sidebar, create a project (e.g. "CTE Work"), and paste your persona into "Edit project instructions." Every new chat you open inside that project auto-loads your persona. You can also upload documents like your department's reference materials so Claude can cite them. — Session 2 Resource Page

"Is it okay to paste actual emails or documents from work into ChatGPT? I'm not sure what's safe to share with an AI."

✅ Top Answer
Great question — treat AI tools like email. You can paste in non-sensitive work content (draft agendas, public policy text, general communications templates) but avoid personally identifiable member information, confidential grievance details, or sensitive HR matters. When in doubt, anonymize first: replace names with "Member A" or "Teacher B." — UFT IT Guidance
